Via Eurasia - an app redesign case study

Introduction

In the ever-evolving landscape of digital experiences, the success of an application hinges not only on functionality but also on the ability to captivate and inspire its users. This case study embarks on a journey to breathe new life into an existing app, which, while functional, has fallen victim to the stagnation of uninspiring design.

Our subject, a once-prominent app, has witnessed a decline in user engagement and enthusiasm. The initial design, while effective, lacks the visual allure and emotional resonance needed to stand out in a competitive market. The goal of this redesign project is to transform the app into an immersive, delightful, and inspiring experience, rekindling user interest and setting a new standard for aesthetic and functional excellence.

Challenges

  • Monotony and Blandness: The current design lacks personality and fails to evoke a sense of excitement or engagement.
  • Outdated Visual Elements: The app's visual elements have become outdated, unable to keep pace with contemporary design trends.
  • User Disengagement: The absence of visual appeal has led to decreased user interest and, consequently, a decline in overall satisfaction and loyalty.

Objectives

  • Inspiration Infusion: Injecting creativity and inspiration into the app's design to foster a stronger emotional connection with users.
  • Modernization: Bringing the visual elements up-to-date with contemporary design aesthetics, ensuring the app remains visually relevant.
  • Enhanced User Engagement: Creating an environment that not only fulfills functional requirements but also encourages prolonged and meaningful user interaction.

Components

The Components Page for the redesigned app serves as a comprehensive guide to the visual and interactive elements that come together to create a harmonious and inspiring user interface. It acts as a repository of meticulously designed components, each playing a crucial role in shaping the overall aesthetic and functionality of the app.

Home Page

The original home page, once functional but uninspiring, is reborn through a comprehensive redesign aimed at captivating users from the moment of entry.

Original home screen was very bland and boring so I added a mountain background to give it life and also updated the two feet logo to be more modern and sleek but still retaining the general look of the original logo. I moved the disclaimer and corresponding logos to the about page because those make more sense being there. I added a nav bar that features the tabs, Home, weather, routes, and about. The home page also features 4 buttons, route selection, route updates, guide book, and points of interest. those 4 button options become encased within a hamburger menu on the other pages of the app. I have decided to use a black and grey glossy color for the main color throughout my redesign, including orange accents that are the same orange as the original screens.

Routes Page

Original routes screen was bland and the pictures going corner to corner just didnt sit right with me for some reason. Also the text was too small and very hard to read. I decided to enlarge the Routes header text at the top of the page, I also enlarged the route titles and distance by a significant amount. I decided to make each route be placed within a card that neatly and cleanly displayed the routes picture, name and description. As stated before I wanted to make the pictures not touch the edges of the screen. I also added the logo in the bottom right corner of each card for an aesthetic look that i think compliments the card rather well.

Routes Detail Page

The original Routes Detail Page presented essential information but lacked the visual appeal and storytelling elements that could elevate the user experience.

Since the original page was lacking life I decided to create a card that was similar to the smaller cards featured on the screen before, only slightly larger and with slightly different features, including a show on map button that in the original screen was awkwardly featured at the bottom of the page where I have the nav bar on my screen. I also improved accessibility by enlarging and bolding the text featured on the page.

Points of Interest Page

Original points of interest page had accessibility issues so I bolded and enlarged the text in the header at the top of the page. I then took the pictures for each point of interest and rounded the edges and added a drop shadow to make the overall appearance of the page more sleek. This page was the one that required the least of a redesign. Just needed a little bit of a sleek modern touch up.

Check Before You Go Page

The check before you go page was deemed unnecessary and not needed so the contents of this screen were placed on the home page, and within the hamburger menu

The decision to eliminate the "Check Before You Go" page and integrate its contents directly onto the home page stems from a strategic reassessment of user needs and the overall user journey within the app. The original "Check Before You Go" page, presumably designed to provide users with pre-departure information or checklists, was perceived as redundant.

About Page

The original about page was bland and lifeless so I used the card style design from previous screens, I also added the flags and logos from the original home page to the bottom of this screen. Overall I believe the appearance of this screen was greatly improved.

Outcomes

After an intensive redesign process aimed at injecting inspiration into an app suffering from a lackluster design, our efforts have culminated in a series of transformative outcomes that not only address the initial challenges but also set a new standard for the apps aesthetic.

Gone are the outdated visual elements that tethered the app to the past. Our redesign embraces contemporary design trends, providing users with an experience that feels fresh, modern, and in tune with the latest industry aesthetics. This evolution positions the app as a trendsetter, rather than a follower, in the competitive landscape.

Lessons Learned

Visual design plays a crucial role in user engagement. Investing in a visually appealing and cohesive design can significantly impact user perception and interaction with the app.

Striking the right balance between aesthetics and functionality is critical. An aesthetically pleasing design should not compromise usability, and functional elements should contribute to the overall visual appeal.

Maintain consistency in branding elements throughout the app. A cohesive visual identity reinforces brand recognition and fosters a sense of trust and familiarity among users.

By reflecting on these lessons, I can distill valuable insights from this case study and apply them to future projects, ensuring a continuous cycle of improvement and innovation in my designs.
